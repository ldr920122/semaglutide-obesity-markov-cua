# Method Precedent Matrix

This matrix compares the current manuscript with recent and closely related obesity pharmacoeconomic studies identified from the manuscript bibliography and PubMed metadata checked on 2026-06-30.

| Study | Method precedent | What it does that is thicker than the current model | Transferable lesson |
|---|---|---|---|
| Jenniskens et al. 2026 | Systematic review for ACP; economic databases; CHEQUE and GRADE assessment | Maps study quality and certainty across pharmacologic obesity CEAs | Frame the current work as transparent, limited, and uncertainty-aware rather than definitive. |
| Altes et al. 2026 | Core Obesity Model v26; 40-year Spanish NHS CEA; DSA and PSA | Uses a recognized obesity-risk-factor Markov platform with BMI, SBP, and glycemic status | Our single-complication model needs explicit limitation language and compensating threshold analysis. |
| Annemans et al. 2026 | Individual-patient simulation; R implementation; T2D progression and OSA remission | Adds OSA, richer T2D progression, discontinuation, convergence and runtime reporting | Full obesity modelling is moving toward multi-complication structures; do not overclaim. |
| McEwan et al. 2025 | SELECT-based cohort Markov model; 100,000-subject simulated cohort | Models CV events, T2D, CKD and CV death | Omitted non-diabetes benefits should be handled through explicit threshold analysis or a future CV model. |
| Rennert-May et al. 2025 | Canadian decision analytic Markov model in obesity with CVD | Monthly cycles and CV event states from a Canadian payer perspective | Canadian threshold and price-reduction framing is acceptable and policy-relevant. |
| Johansson et al. 2026 | Patient-level simulation using SURMOUNT-5 | Models CVD, OSA and other obesity-related complications with cardiometabolic risk factors | Current model is more transparent but structurally simpler; novelty must rest on reproducibility and value-frontier analysis. |
| Current manuscript | Public-source cohort Markov reference model | Adds DSA, PSA, CEAC, retained-effect scenarios, price thresholds, unmodeled-benefit frontier, PSA driver correlations and EVPI | After the enhancement, method thickness is acceptable for Frontiers-style health economics but still not a full HTA. |

## Bottom Line

Before enhancement, the manuscript was scientifically coherent but structurally thin relative to the newest obesity economic-modelling literature. After adding the unmodeled-benefit threshold frontier and PSA driver analysis, the manuscript has a stronger methodological defense: it does not pretend to be a full cardiovascular/OSA obesity model, but it quantifies how large omitted benefits would need to be to change the value conclusion.
